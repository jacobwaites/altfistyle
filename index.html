LOGO SPACING GUIDELINES

when using the logo we try to keep equidistant spacing around the logo. Space around the logo should be a minimum of .25in (actually a little more, like .39in) or 2.375rem on web (with a 16px root). Or more! Just keep things equal.

COLOR USAGE

When using colors that are this saturated it is key to remember control of contrast, avoid colors touching unless they are part of an intricate pattern where they are used for dramatic effect, or on large swaths of empty space where the viewer has room to breathe. when using type on the colors, remember to create as much contrast as possible, and to not let two colors touch in ways that decrease readability until it's completely unreadable (unless that's what you're going for of course).

Photography Treatment

Photos from the future

PHOTO TREATMENT & STYLES

The photography used in any project can vary in style, but we have a few base styles that you can use if you're looking to make content quickly branded.

THE TINT

The first style is an 80% tint (at least, go even further for more contrast) on either a #222 or a #0600fa background. This is quick and effective, and will make white text much more readable in presentations.

CHANNEL SPLIT

The second style is a channel split after either converting the image to CMYK, or doing some transparency tricks in photoshop. Either way, you should have three layers of the same image with the multiply blending mode on them. The layers are then randomly nudged out of their aligned positions, reminiscent of a registration error when printing in the CMYK color space. The appropriation of an anachronistic medium results in a juxtaposition of how the colab views the past, as something to be referenced, learned from, and remixed.

LOGO TREATMENT

Placing the CoLab logo onto photographs should be done with care (we gotta look good). The CoLab logo can be used as a centered object on a photo, large or small. It can encroach on space when necessary, or exist in negative space. Or you can tuck it into the corner of a photo (we like to keep about .25in off the edge). The key thing to remember is contrast, if the photo is dark, use a white CoLab logo, if it's light, use a dark grey CoLab logo. On complicated photos, you may opt to place a white box around the logo, if you do this, make sure the white box leaves equidistant space around all of the edges of the logo.
